Your Laser Eye Operation in 3 Steps

Pre-operative assessment, intervention, and post-operative follow-up: find all the practical information about your refractive surgery at Eye Clinic.

1

A Pre-Operative Assessment to Rule Out All Contraindications

The Examinations Performed

Before any treatment, a pre-operative assessment must be carried out. This involves a complete ophthalmological examination at Eye Clinic's surgical office. The examinations are non-invasive and painless. They typically include a measurement of the thickness and curvature of your cornea, the size of your pupils, your visual acuity, and the length of your eyeball.

This consultation lasts approximately 3 hours, during which the ophthalmologic surgeon will determine if there are any contraindications and whether you are eligible for refractive surgery. They will provide the diagnosis and the laser technique to consider. If you are suitable, and you consent, they will write a post-operative prescription and schedule the procedure.

The assessment also includes a dilated eye examination to verify the proper functioning of the retina. We simply instil a few dilating eye drops into your eyes to dilate your pupils before taking a series of measurements. You will be sensitive to light and your vision will be blurry for a few hours. A temporary tingling sensation may be felt from the eye drops.

Practical Information

  • Allow 2h30 of your time
  • Plan to come accompanied or not to drive during the 4 hours following the consultation
  • If you wear contact lenses, remove them: 5 days before for soft lenses, 3 weeks for rigid lenses
  • Bring your most recent prescription for glasses or contact lenses
  • Wear sunglasses for your comfort — you will be more light-sensitive during the dilation of the eye exam
2

The Day of the Intervention

How does the day of laser surgery actually work? We explain everything.

1. Change of Clothing

Before entering the surgical room, our team will give you a "sterile" outfit — a cap for your hair, a gown to cover your clothing, and shoe covers. Do not wear make-up or contact lenses several days before the procedure.

2. Local Anaesthesia

Anaesthesia is achieved through a few drops of anaesthetic eye drops instilled into both eyes. They take effect in seconds. There is no general anaesthesia and the procedure is painless. We also place a speculum to keep your eyelids open.

3. 20 Minutes of Intervention

The intervention lasts around 20 minutes and you will remain in our company throughout. You can find all the details of laser surgery procedures on our dedicated techniques pages. Plan to come accompanied and rest for at least 2 hours once home.

4. Start Your Recovery

Post-operative care begins on the same day, following the prescription from your doctor. We provide you with sunglasses and protective eye shields for the first few nights.

3

Post-Operative Care

After the procedure, we advise you to sleep for at least 3 hours, simply because when your eyes are closed they are healing more quickly. You should also wear the sunglasses that we provide on the first day.

With LASIK and SMILE techniques, you can return to work and drive from the next day. It is perfectly normal to feel a slight discomfort, like a foreign body sensation in the eye, during the first hours. It is also possible to experience some blurriness during the first few days.

There are several recommendations to follow: eye drops will be prescribed according to your situation by the doctor. In general, avoid rubbing your eyes during the first days, do not go swimming for 3 weeks. You will also follow a post-operative prescription of antibiotic and anti-inflammatory eye drops and artificial tears for several days.

In the majority of cases, the first check-up is the day after your refractive surgery, at the ophthalmologic surgeon's office. A follow-up is then performed over several months.
