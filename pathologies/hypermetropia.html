What Is Hypermetropia?

Hypermetropia, or farsightedness, is a common visual condition where nearby objects appear blurry while distant objects may still be seen more clearly. It affects approximately 10% of the population.

The condition occurs when the eye is too short or the cornea is too flat, causing light to focus behind the retina rather than directly on it. In younger people, the lens can compensate by accommodating, but this becomes increasingly difficult with age.

Symptoms include difficulty reading, eye fatigue, headaches after prolonged close work, and a feeling of strain. Hypermetropia often coexists with presbyopia in patients over 40.

Unlike myopia, mild hypermetropia may go unnoticed in young adults. However, as the lens loses its flexibility with age, the condition becomes more apparent and problematic.

Treating Hypermetropia with Laser Surgery

Laser surgery for hypermetropia works by increasing the curvature of the cornea, allowing light to focus correctly on the retina. The procedure reshapes the central cornea to make it steeper.

At Eye Clinic, the most common techniques for hypermetropia include:

  • LASIK — The gold standard for hypermetropia correction up to +6 dioptres
  • PRK — Surface treatment for patients with thinner corneas
  • Implantable lenses — For higher prescriptions or when laser is not suitable

Both eyes are typically treated in the same session, and most patients experience significant improvement within 24 to 48 hours.
