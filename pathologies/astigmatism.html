What Is Astigmatism?

Astigmatism is an extremely common visual defect affecting approximately one in three people. It causes blurred or distorted vision at all distances — both near and far.

The condition is caused by an irregularly shaped cornea. Instead of being round like a football, the astigmatic cornea is shaped more like a rugby ball. This irregular curvature prevents light from focusing evenly on the retina, creating two different focal points.

Astigmatism is often present from birth and frequently occurs alongside myopia or hypermetropia. Symptoms include blurred vision, eye strain, headaches, and difficulty with night driving.

Mild astigmatism may not require treatment, but moderate to severe cases benefit significantly from surgical correction.

Treating Astigmatism with Laser Surgery

Laser refractive surgery is highly effective at correcting astigmatism. The laser reshapes the cornea to create a more uniform curvature, allowing light to focus properly on the retina.

At Eye Clinic, we offer multiple approaches depending on the severity of your astigmatism:

  • LASIK — The most popular technique. A thin corneal flap is created before reshaping the underlying tissue.
  • SMILE Pro — For combined myopia and astigmatism. Minimally invasive with faster recovery.
  • PRK (Surface Treatment) — Ideal for patients with thin corneas.

Astigmatism can be treated alone or simultaneously with myopia, hypermetropia, or presbyopia in a single procedure.
