What Is Presbyopia?

Presbyopia is a natural, age-related condition that affects everyone, typically beginning around the age of 40. It results from the gradual loss of flexibility in the crystalline lens inside the eye.

As the lens becomes less elastic, it loses its ability to change shape and focus on nearby objects. This is why reading becomes increasingly difficult, and you may find yourself holding books or your phone at arm's length.

Presbyopia is not a disease — it is a natural part of ageing. It continues to progress until around age 60, when the lens has lost most of its accommodative ability. It affects 100% of the population over time.

Presbyopia can occur on its own or in combination with other refractive errors such as myopia, hypermetropia, or astigmatism.

Managing Presbyopia Professionally

At Eye Clinic, we offer advanced clinical management for presbyopia, focusing on giving you back your visual freedom without the constant need for reading glasses.

We provide multiple professional options based on your specific visual needs and ocular health:

  • Multifocal Spectacle Lenses — Advanced varifocals that offer seamless transitions between distance, intermediate, and near vision.
  • Contemporary Multifocal Contact Lenses — Modern lens materials that allow freedom from frames while providing clear all-round vision.
  • De-Stressed Vision Training — Advice on lighting and visual ergonomics to help you cope with changing near-vision requirements.

The best option depends on your age, lifestyle, and visual goals. Our team will perform a detailed clinical evaluation to recommend the most effective solution for you.
