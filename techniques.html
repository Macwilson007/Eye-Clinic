Our Surgical Techniques

Eye Clinic offers the most advanced laser and surgical techniques for refractive surgery. Discover which one is right for you.

LATEST TECHNOLOGY

SMILE Pro

Small Incision Lenticule Extraction — The Future of Refractive Surgery

SMILE Pro represents the third generation of refractive surgery, following PRK and LASIK. It is the most advanced, minimally invasive technique available today.

How It Works

A femtosecond laser creates a thin discoid lenticule within the intact cornea. This lenticule is then removed through a tiny 2-4mm incision — no flap creation is needed.

Advantages

  • Flapless — no risk of flap-related complications
  • Minimally invasive — only a small incision
  • Faster recovery with less dry eye
  • Better corneal biomechanical stability
  • Procedure completed in under 30 seconds per eye

Treats

Myopia (up to -10D) and Astigmatism (up to -5D)

GOLD STANDARD

LASIK

Laser-Assisted In-Situ Keratomileusis — The Most Popular Technique Worldwide

LASIK is the most widely performed refractive surgery procedure worldwide, with millions of successful treatments. It remains an excellent choice for a wide range of prescriptions.

How It Works

A femtosecond laser creates a thin corneal flap. The flap is lifted, and an excimer laser reshapes the underlying corneal tissue to correct the refractive error. The flap is then repositioned.

Advantages

  • Rapid visual recovery — often within hours
  • Minimal discomfort during and after the procedure
  • Treats a very wide range of prescriptions
  • Proven track record of safety and efficacy
  • Can treat myopia, hypermetropia, astigmatism & presbyopia

Treats

Myopia (up to -8D), Hypermetropia (up to +6D), Astigmatism (up to -5D), and Presbyopia

PRK / TransPRK

Photorefractive Keratectomy — Surface Laser Treatment

PRK was the first laser refractive surgery technique developed and remains an important option, particularly for patients whose corneas are too thin for LASIK or SMILE.

How It Works

The outer layer of the cornea (epithelium) is gently removed, and an excimer laser reshapes the underlying cornea. With TransPRK, even the epithelial removal is performed by the laser — making it a fully contactless procedure.

Advantages

  • No flap creation — ideal for thin corneas
  • Suitable for patients with active lifestyles or contact sports
  • TransPRK is fully contactless — no instrument touches the eye
  • Long-term results comparable to LASIK

Note: Recovery takes slightly longer than LASIK (3-5 days of discomfort), but the final visual outcome is excellent.
